## Memorandum 4-A  
### Preliminary Assessment of the Hypothesis That Lunar Landings Occurred

**Division:** Astronomical Verification Directorate  
**Series:** Extraterrestrial Operational Review  

**Summary:**  
This memorandum evaluates the plausibility of the hypothesis that the United States successfully conducted crewed missions to the Moon between 1969 and 1972.

Evidence cited includes:

- lunar rock samples  
- laser reflectors on the lunar surface  
- extensive telemetry records  
- video documentation  
- cultural references including the 1992 R.E.M. song *Man on the Moon*

The analysis also notes that film technology of the late 1960s may have been insufficient to convincingly fabricate such events.

Conclusion:

> The United States won the Cold War.  
> If the Lunar Landing Hypothesis holds true, the United States won the Space Race.

# III. Lunar Continuity Program Arc

## Memorandum: Lunar Continuity Planning

**Theme:** Civilizational redundancy.

**Summary:**  
Documents suggest the creation of a concealed human settlement on the far side of the Moon intended to preserve humanity in the event of nuclear war.

The settlement reportedly features:

- universal healthcare  
- absence of crime  
- high reported life satisfaction

Earth authorities eventually request admission to the program.

Shortly thereafter, the lunar settlement becomes unreachable.
