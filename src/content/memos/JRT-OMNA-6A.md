## I. Situation Overview

Recent analysis by the **Office of Macroeconomic Narrative Accounting (OMNA)** indicates that traditional national accounting categories no longer adequately describe the primary drivers of economic activity within the contemporary economy.

Although Gross Domestic Product continues to be reported using legacy industrial classifications, observational evidence suggests that a growing share of economic output now originates in sectors better described as **narrative, regulatory, or anxiety-management industries**.

OMNA therefore conducted a provisional review of alternative GDP sector classifications designed to reflect the statistically emergent structure of the modern economy. These revisions address longstanding issues of **legacy accounting inertia**, in which national accounts continued to rely on categories preserved largely because the reporting forms still contained those boxes.

---

## II. Background

Traditional GDP accounting divides economic activity into broad sectors including agriculture, manufacturing, services, and government.

Several high-growth sectors now generate economic activity primarily through **expectation management, legal mediation, regulatory compliance, brand signaling, and narrative amplification**. These activities represent an advanced stage of the post-industrial service economy, in which value increasingly derives from coordination, signaling, and expectation calibration rather than from physical production.

To evaluate the relative importance of these activities, the committee prepared a provisional distribution of GDP across the revised sector framework, presented in Table 1.

### Non-Disruptor Legacy Production

This category includes firms still operating within the United States engaged in the manufacture of physical goods, including machinery, industrial materials, chemicals, and logistical equipment.

Despite their operational importance, these sectors exhibit **limited narrative visibility** and therefore appear infrequently in venture capital discourse except in **vulture fund quarterly disclosures**. Their output remains stable but statistically overshadowed by sectors with higher narrative velocity.

---

### Meme Construction

This sector encompasses industries responsible for the creation and amplification of economic narratives, including digital media platforms, advertising and marketing services, influencer networks, long-form conversational podcasting, algorithmic content distribution systems, and speculative retail investment communities.

Economic activity derives from the circulation of **memetic narratives—investment theses, consumer trends, lifestyle advice, and macroeconomic interpretations—processed through a multi-stage narrative supply chain.**

Upstream inputs include institutional commentary, administrative announcements, and algorithmically surfaced sentiment fragments. Downstream processes include amplification, remixing, and distribution through high-velocity attention markets.

Several publicly traded firms have experienced substantial valuation fluctuations attributable primarily to meme amplification cycles. Many now maintain **strategic Meme Reserves** to stabilize valuation during periods of narrative scarcity. **Derivative Meme Instruments (DMIs)** have emerged as hedging tools for managing meme decay risk.

---

### Wellness and Looksmaxxing

This sector includes industries devoted to personal optimization, including cosmetic procedures, dermatology, supplements, fitness services, and lifestyle enhancement products.

The category also includes **licensed cannabis dispensaries**, which provide plant-based stress-management products increasingly marketed within the broader wellness economy.

Output is measured in **perceived self-improvement units**, reflecting the sector’s role in maintaining labor-force readiness within a biopsychosocial productivity enhancement regime.

---

### Health Risk Monitoring

Historically referred to as **health care**, this sector increasingly encompasses activities devoted to the identification and continuous monitoring of potential health risks.

Expansion reflects improved diagnostic capabilities and the growing ability to detect previously unnoticed conditions.

A portion of demand appears driven by consumers seeking reassurance regarding conditions **that may or may not currently exist**. **Pre-Diagnostic Vigilance Services**, monitoring for conditions not yet medically defined, represent a rapidly expanding subsector.

---

### Organic Agricultural Signaling

This sector includes agricultural production accompanied by certification systems designed to communicate environmental or ethical attributes.

A growing share of production involves **conventional fertilized and chemically supported agricultural processes** that have successfully obtained organic certification through administrative compliance pathways.

In some jurisdictions, **certification bodies now outnumber certified farms.**

---

### Monetary Expectation Management

This category includes central banking institutions and financial markets engaged in stabilizing macroeconomic expectations around **nominal wage increases modestly below prevailing inflation rates**.

Within current policy frameworks, the central bank functions as a **guarantor of Nominal Wage Stability**.

Operational objectives frequently align with several informal mandates, including:

- **Nominal Wage Stability**  
- **Consumer Credit Rate Normalization**  
- **Capital Market Wealth Continuity**

Forward Guidance Mood Boards are periodically released to maintain sentiment alignment. Certain digital assets intermittently function as **auxiliary expectation-management instruments** within the broader monetary environment.

---

### Litigation Services

This sector includes legal services devoted to resolving disputes arising from commercial activity, regulatory interpretation, intellectual property conflicts, and professional liability.

Litigation converts commercial disagreements into **billable hours** and remains a stable contributor to GDP.

---

### Government

Government continues to represent a substantial share of GDP and requires **no interpretive clarification at this time**.

## IV. Market Observations

Several firms now command market capitalizations comparable to major industrial companies despite producing limited physical output. This reflects the increasing economic role of **collective narrative formation**, particularly within retail trading communities.

Recent GDP expansion appears increasingly concentrated within the **Meme Construction sector**, while most legacy sectors remain structurally stable but largely non-expansive.

Analysts within OMNA have begun referring to the emerging analytical framework underlying these dynamics as **Supply-Side Memenomics (SSM)**, a model in which sustained economic expansion depends on the continuous production and distribution of narrative content capable of supporting investor sentiment, consumer confidence, and market liquidity.

A prolonged decline in meme generation—internally referred to as a **“meme drought”**—could introduce recessionary conditions, including reduced employment growth and delayed labor-market recovery. Comparable dynamics were observed during the **2008 recession and subsequent jobless recovery**.

The **Meme Survival Period (MSP)**—the duration for which asset prices remain stable in the absence of new narrative content—is currently estimated at **three to five news cycles**.

## V. Statistical Rebasing of the Narrative Economy

Application of the revised sector framework results in a measured increase in national economic output of **approximately 20.3 percent** relative to earlier GDP calculations. This adjustment does not represent newly created economic activity but rather the recognition of value previously omitted from official measurement.

Updated methodology incorporates measurement approaches designed to capture previously underrepresented forms of output, including:

- narrative amplification services  
- multi-platform meme propagation  
- influencer-driven consumption signaling  
- digital attention-market activity  

The largest contribution to the revision derives from the **Meme Construction sector**, followed by expanded estimates for Wellness and Looksmaxxing and narrative-adjacent financial activity.

Rebasing required retroactive reinterpretation of historical data, resulting in revised GDP estimates for years predating the invention of memes. Adjustments were made using the **Narrative Deflator**, which corrects for excessive optimism or pessimism in historical sentiment archives.

Early tests indicate that several historical recessions were partially attributable to **narrative contraction events not previously captured in official statistics**. Under the updated methodology, the **2020 COVID recession would have been provisionally classified as a period of elevated narrative activity.**

## VI. Advisory Status

Traditional GDP categories remain operationally valid but require **interpretive supplementation** to accurately describe contemporary economic structures.

Continued monitoring of sectors within the **Narrative Economy—particularly those associated with meme construction, narrative velocity, and amplification half-life—is recommended.**

OMNA will continue refining the statistical architecture necessary to ensure that national accounting remains aligned with the evolving **narrative substrate of the economy**.

Further study is warranted.
