## I. OBJECTIVE

Following formal acknowledgment of its existence, the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) commissioned a controlled perception analysis to evaluate alternative public-facing terminology.

Goal:

- Maintain mystique  
- Reduce scrutiny  
- Avoid sounding like a committee

---

## II. TESTED TERMINOLOGY

Participants were presented with the following naming options:

1. Central Intelligence Agency  
2. The Agency  
3. Directorate K  
4. The Directorate  
5. Office of Strategic Continuity  

Participants:

- Male  
- Ages 43–59  
- █████████ suburban homeowners  
- Own at least one grill  
- Use the phrase *“back in my day”* unironically  

---

## III. RESULTS

| Term | Favorability | Emotional Reaction |
|-----|-------------|-------------------|
| Central Intelligence Agency | 6.1 | “Sounds official.” |
| The Agency | 9.3 | “That’s solid.” |
| Directorate K | 9.1 | “Feels serious.” |
| The Directorate | 7.4 | “European.” |
| Office of Strategic Continuity | 3.2 | “Is that insurance?” |

“Directorate K” scored highly in perceived decisiveness and operational gravity.

However, participants described it as:

- “A little intense.”  
- “Feels like someone disappears.”  
- “Probably has a basement.”  

The term was therefore categorized as:

Operationally Effective / Publicly Uncomfortable

---

## IV. ETHICAL REVIEW

The Internal Committee on Democratic Presentation concluded:

While “Directorate K” evokes discipline and quiet authority, its adoption would exceed the current level of democratic maturity appropriate for public institutions that describe themselves as “the good guys.”

Accordingly, the CIA affirms:

> Because the United States is widely understood to be the good guys, the use of a letter is unnecessary.

The letter **“K”** has therefore been retired from consideration in this context.

---

## V. FINAL TERMINOLOGY GUIDANCE

For public reference:

- **“The Agency”** is preferred.  
- **“Directorate K”** is reserved for internal motivational use and certain hallway whispers.

The official statutory name remains **Central Intelligence Agency**.
