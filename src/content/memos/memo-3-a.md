## I. PURPOSE

This memorandum formally acknowledges the existence of the Central Intelligence Agency following sustained administrative pressure resulting from cumulative requests submitted under the Freedom of Information Act.

After extensive internal review, the Agency determined that maintaining non-acknowledgment required more documentation than existence itself.

The Agency therefore confirms:

1. It exists.  
2. It has existed.  
3. Its prior non-acknowledgment was procedural.

________________________________________  

## II. STRATEGIC TERMINOLOGY

While the official designation remains Central Intelligence Agency (CIA), the public is encouraged, for purposes of operational mystique and narrative efficiency, to refer to the CIA simply as:

**“The Agency.”**

Use of this terminology preserves ambiguity while maintaining brand continuity.

## V. CONGRESSIONAL RESPONSE

Following public acknowledgment of the Agency’s existence, the United States Congress convened hearings to determine whether such an entity had been lawfully established.

After brief confusion and review of several binders labeled *“We Assumed It Existed,”* Congress passed on April 1, 1978 the:

**Central Intelligence Agency Establishment and Retroactive Authorization Act**

This legislation formally creates the CIA, effective:

**September 18, 1947.**

This date was selected for administrative convenience.

Accordingly:

• The CIA now exists by statute.  
• It existed prior to statute.  
• It will continue existing regardless of statute.

Certain archival documents referencing the Agency prior to 1947 remain under review.

The temporal discrepancy has been classified as **“Resolved.”**
